What I'm building
octivis.com ↗

Every store already has cameras. Octivis makes them useful.

Octivis is a computer-vision platform that reads the feeds retailers already run and turns them into the handful of answers a manager actually acts on — how long the lines are, where shoppers stall, when to open another register. No new hardware to install, no dashboard nobody opens.

  • Uses cameras you already own. Nothing new to bolt to the ceiling.
  • Built on AWS. One store or a whole chain, same system.
  • Privacy by design. It reads patterns and movement, not identities.

June 2026 – Present

Embassy of Pakistan · Washington, DC

Building AI tools for an environment where the data can't leak — private and local LLM deployments, retrieval that respects access control, and the unglamorous security work underneath: least-privilege access, encryption, IAM, audit logging. Good practice for a founder who'll be trusted with other people's data.

May 2026 – Present

Graduate Archer Fellow — AI Policy

UT System Archer Fellowship · Washington, DC

A competitive fellowship placing UT graduate students in Washington. My work looks at the parts of AI most builders skip — the energy and infrastructure cost of running models at scale, and how governance actually gets written. I translate between engineers and the people who make policy, and I'm better at both for it.

Sep 2022 – May 2025

Machine Learning Researcher

UT Arlington · Machine Learning Lab

Three years of computer-vision and robotics research — object detection and tracking, reinforcement-learning locomotion, real-time perception with PyTorch, OpenCV and YOLO. The core tech behind Octivis started here. Recognized at UTA Innovation Day (Judges' Choice '24, 3rd Place '23) and the Student Computing Research Festival (Best Presentation '24).
